Brownie:

Ingridients and Subsititutions

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
    ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter (or vegetable oil)
  • melted ¼ cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup chocolate chips (optional for extra fudginess)
  • ¼ cup chopped nuts (optional)

Instructions

  • Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease an 8x8-inch baking pan or line it with parchment paper.
  • Combine
  • Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt until well combined.
  • Add Wet Ingredients: Pour in the melted butter (or oil), milk, and vanilla extract. Mix until just combined, being careful not to overmix. The batter will be thick.
  • Fold in Chocolate Chips & Nuts: Gently fold in the chocolate chips and nuts if you’re using them.
  • Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread it out evenly. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs. Avoid over-baking to keep the brownies fudgy.
  • Cool & Serve: Let the brownies cool in the pan before cutting into squares.

Enjoy! These brownies are great as they are, but you can add a sprinkle of powdered sugar on top or serve them with ice cream for an extra treat!!!

Tiramisu:

Ingridients and Subsititutions

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup sugar (150g)
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese (225g)
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream (360ml)
  • 1 1/2 cups espresso or strong coffee, cooled
  • 2 tbsp coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 packs ladyfingers (savoiardi biscuits)
  • Cocoa powder for dusting
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional)

Instructions

  • Prepare the Coffee Mixture: Brew espresso or strong coffee. Let it cool, and add the coffee liqueur if you're using it. Set aside
  • Make the Mascarpone Cream: In a large bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until the mixture is thick and pale, about 3-5 minutes. Gently fold in the mascarpone cheese and vanilla extract until smooth.
  • Whip the Cream: In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixtureuntil combined.
  • Layer the Tiramisu: Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the cooled coffee, soaking them for about 1-2 seconds per side (don’t oversoak). Arrange a layer of coffee-dipped ladyfingers in the bottom of a 9x13-inch dish or another serving dish. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.
  • Repeat Layers: Add a second layer of soaked ladyfingers, then top with the remaining mascarpone cream. Chill the Tiramisu: Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld.
  • Dust and Serve: Just before serving, dust the top with cocoa powder. Add chocolate shavings for extra flair, if desired.

Enjoy your homemade tiramisu!!!

Ricotta Cheesecake:

Ingridients and Subsititutions

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs (about 10-12 crackers)
  • 3 tbsp sugar 6 tbsp butter, melted Cheesecake
  • 1 ½ cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups cream cheese (room temperature)
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp lemon zest (finely grated)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour Pinch of salt
  • Fresh berries (strawberries, raspberries, blueberries)
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions

  • Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  • Prepare the Crust: In a medium bowl, mix together the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter until the crumbs are evenly coated and moist. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then set it aside to cool.
  • Make the Cheesecake Filling: In a large bowl, beat the ricotta and cream cheese together until smooth and creamy. Add the sugar and beat until fully incorporated. Mix in the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Add the vanilla extract, lemon zest, lemon juice, flour, and salt. Beat until smooth and combined.
  • Assemble the Cheesecake: Pour the cheesecake filling over the cooled crust in the springform pan, smoothing the top with a spatula..
  • Bake the Cheesecake: Bake in the preheated oven for 55-65 minutes, or until the cheesecake is set but still slightly wobbly in the center. Turn off the oven and leave the cheesecake inside with the door slightly open for 1 hour to cool gradually (this helps prevent cracking).
  • Chill: Remove the cheesecake from the oven and let it cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight for the best results.
  • Serve: When ready to serve, remove the cheesecake from the springform pan. Garnish with fresh berries or dust with powdered sugar if desired.

Enjoy your creamy and light Ricotta Cheesecake!!!!

Caramel Panna Cotta:

Ingridients and Subsititutions

    For the Caramel Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (100g)
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream (60ml)
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Pinch of salt (optional, for salted caramel)

    • For the Panna Cotta:

  • 1 cup heavy cream (240ml)
  • 1 cup whole milk (240ml)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (100g)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tsp gelatin powder
  • 3 tbsp water (to dissolve gelatin)

Instructions

    Make the Caramel Sauce:

  • Cook the Caramel: In a medium saucepan, combine the sugar and 2 tbsp water. Cook over medium heat, swirling the pan (don’t stir) until the sugar melts and turns into a deep amber color. This usually takes 5-7 minutes.
  • Add Cream and Butter: Remove from heat and slowly whisk in the heavy cream (be careful, as it will bubble up). Stir in the butter until fully melted and smooth. If you want salted caramel, add a pinch of salt now. Set the caramel aside to cool slightly.

    • Make the Panna Cotta:

  • Prepare Gelatin: In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin over 3 tablespoons of cold water. Let it sit for about 5 minutes to bloom (absorb the water).
  • Heat the Cream: In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, milk, and sugar. Heat over medium heat until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture is hot but not boiling.
  • Add Gelatin: Remove the cream mixture from heat, and stir in the bloomed gelatin until it fully dissolves. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  • Assemble the Panna Cotta: Pour a spoonful of the caramel sauce into the bottom of each serving glass or mold. Carefully pour the warm panna cotta mixture over the caramel sauce.
  • Chill: Cover the glasses with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or until fully set (overnight is ideal).
  • Serve: To serve, you can either eat the panna cotta directly from the glass or unmold it onto a plate. To unmold, dip the bottom of the mold briefly in hot water, then invert onto a plate. Garnish with extra caramel sauce if desired.
